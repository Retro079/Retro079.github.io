How to Use These Tools

Each tool below is designed to help you explore your identity and share your story in creative ways. Click the links to access the tools directly.

MusicFX DJ

Google Creative Tool

Create a short beat that represents your background, then share your "Where I'm From" story over it.

Student Instructions:

  1. Click the button below to open the MusicFX DJ tool
  2. Create a short beat that feels like your background or where you're from
  3. Think about your "Where I'm From" story
  4. When it's your turn, play your beat and share your story over it
  5. Listen for common threads in other students' stories
Example: "Where I'm from, most kids don't know how to read. But when I was 7, my mom would sit me down and make me read books after dinner."
Open MusicFX DJ Tool

Whisk (Art Creation)

Google Creative Tool

Create a digital self-portrait or "Picasso" that represents your identity when words aren't enough.

Student Instructions:

  1. Open the Whisk tool using the link below
  2. Think: "If you could make a painting to introduce yourself, what would it be?"
  3. Create a drawing or design that represents your identity
  4. Be prepared to explain your choices to the group
  5. Try to match other students' drawings to the artists during the activity
Tip: Your drawing can be abstract - it doesn't have to look like you. It should represent who you are inside.
Open Whisk Tool

Doppl (Style & Outfits)

Google Creative Tool

Design an outfit for a professional setting and explain how it reflects who you are.

Student Instructions:

  1. Open the Doppl tool using the link below
  2. Break into small groups if instructed
  3. Design or describe an outfit for: job interview, networking event, or formal occasion
  4. Present your outfit to the group and explain how it reflects your identity
  5. Discuss why presentation matters in leadership
Remember: At Morehouse, presentation matters. How you present yourself connects to confidence and how people see you.

Workshop Activities & Games

Interactive exercises for building brotherhood, exploring identity, and creating impact

Where I'm From

Identity Activity

What You'll Do:

Create a short beat that represents your background, then share your "Where I'm From" story over it.

Student Instructions:

  1. Listen to the facilitator's example first
  2. Create your beat using the MusicFX DJ tool
  3. Start your story with "Where I'm from..." and share something meaningful
  4. Play your beat and share your story when it's your turn
  5. Listen actively to others' stories - look for commonalities

Who I'm Not

Identity Activity

What You'll Do:

Flip negative self-statements into positive affirmations.

Student Instructions:

  1. Think of a negative thing you sometimes say about yourself
  2. Flip it into a positive statement (Example: "I'm not weak â†’ I am strong")
  3. Share your positive statement with the group when ready
  4. The group will celebrate each positive statement
  5. Really try to believe the positive statement about yourself

Pick Your Picasso

Identity Activity

What You'll Do:

Create an art piece that represents your identity.

Student Instructions:

  1. Get an index card and pen/pencil if not using the digital tool
  2. Draw a tattoo you would get or a self-portrait that represents you
  3. You have 2-3 minutes to create your "Picasso"
  4. All cards will be collected and redistributed
  5. Try to match the drawings to the artists in the room
  6. Explain your drawing when it's returned to you

Fear For Me

Brotherhood Activity

What You'll Do:

Share a fear and receive support from others.

Student Instructions:

  1. Think of one fear you're comfortable sharing
  2. When it's your turn, share your fear with the group
  3. The next person will respond with support, encouragement, or advice
  4. Listen carefully when others share - offer genuine support
  5. Remember: What's said here, stays here

If You Knew Me

Brotherhood Activity

What You'll Do:

Complete the sentence "If you really knew me, you'd know..."

Student Instructions:

  1. Listen to the facilitator's example first
  2. Complete the sentence: "If you really knew me, you'd know..."
  3. Share something meaningful about yourself
  4. Go around the circle - you can pass if you need to
  5. Push yourself to share something deeper if you're comfortable

The Line Game

Brotherhood Activity

What You'll Do:

Step forward if a statement applies to you.

Student Instructions:

  1. Stand on one side of the tape line on the floor
  2. Listen carefully to each question read by the facilitator
  3. If the statement applies to you, step forward over the line
  4. Look around to see who else steps forward
  5. Questions will range from light to deeper
  6. You don't have to explain why you stepped forward
Example Questions: "Do you own a pair of Jordans?" "Do you live with both parents?" "Do you know someone in a gang?"

Spotlight on Change Agents

Action & Impact

What You'll Do:

Research and discuss someone making a difference.

Student Instructions:

  1. Research or think about someone making a difference (local, national, or global)
  2. Prepare to present this person to the group
  3. Share: Who they are, what they do, why they inspire you
  4. Listen to others' presentations
  5. Discuss what makes an effective change agent

Protecting Our Future

Action & Impact

What You'll Do:

Write letters supporting funding for HBCUs and minority-serving institutions.

Student Instructions:

  1. Get paper and pen
  2. Write a letter to a representative supporting funding for HBCUs, HSIs, TCUs, and MSIs

Student Questions & Answers

Common questions from students participating in the workshops for the first time

What if I'm nervous about sharing my story in "Where I'm From"?

It's completely normal to feel nervous! Remember that everyone is sharing something personal. Start with something simple - it doesn't have to be your deepest secret. The goal is to find connections with others, not to tell your entire life story. Most students find that once they start sharing, the nerves go away.

Do I have to be good at drawing for "Pick Your Picasso"?

Not at all! This isn't an art contest. Your drawing can be simple shapes, symbols, or even just colors that represent you. The focus is on what the drawing means to you, not how well it's drawn. Many students use abstract symbols that have personal meaning.

What if I can't think of a fear to share in "Fear For Me"?

It's okay to share something small or simple! It doesn't have to be a major fear. Examples could be: fear of public speaking, fear of not doing well in school, or even fear of trying something new. The activity is about building trust, not about having the "biggest" fear.

What happens if I don't want to answer a question in "The Line Game"?

You can choose not to step forward for any question. No one will ask why you didn't step forward. The game is about finding connections, not putting anyone on the spot. You only share what you're comfortable with.

Do I need to bring anything for these activities?

No, everything is provided! For the digital tools, you'll use the provided devices or your own phone if you prefer. For art activities, paper and pens are provided. Just bring your willingness to participate and an open mind!

What if I don't know anyone at the workshop?

That's perfect! Most students come in not knowing anyone. The activities are specifically designed to help you get to know others in a comfortable way. By the end of the first activity, you'll already have things in common with other students.
