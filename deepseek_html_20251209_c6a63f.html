Our Historic Journey

From 1867 to Today: A Legacy of Excellence

1867

The Foundation

Morehouse College was founded in Augusta, Georgia, as the Augusta Institute. Established by William Jefferson White with the support of the Rev. Richard C. Coulter and the Rev. Edmund Turney, the institution was founded to educate African American men in theology and education.

1906

Becoming Morehouse

The institution was renamed Morehouse College in honor of Henry L. Morehouse, corresponding secretary of the American Baptist Home Mission Society. The college moved to its current location in Atlanta, Georgia.

1940

Benjamin Mays Era

Dr. Benjamin E. Mays became president, serving for 27 years. During his tenure, he mentored Martin Luther King Jr. and transformed Morehouse into a leading institution for Black leadership and civil rights activism.

1968

MLK Memorial

Morehouse held the funeral for its most famous alumnus, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., Class of 1948. The ceremony was held on the college's campus, drawing dignitaries from around the world.

Present

Modern Excellence

Today, Morehouse continues its mission as the nation's only historically Black liberal arts college for men. With state-of-the-art facilities, renowned faculty, and a commitment to producing leaders, Morehouse remains at the forefront of higher education.

Our Mission & Core Values

The Principles That Guide Every Morehouse Man

Academic Excellence

We challenge students to achieve the highest standards of intellectual engagement and scholarly achievement in a rigorous liberal arts environment.

Servant Leadership

We develop men who use their knowledge and talents to serve their communities and lead with integrity, compassion, and social responsibility.

Brotherhood

We foster a lifelong bond among students, alumni, faculty, and staff that creates a supportive network of men committed to each other's success.

Social Justice

We continue the legacy of our alumni by preparing students to confront injustice and work toward creating a more equitable society.

Critical Inquiry

We encourage intellectual curiosity, critical thinking, and the pursuit of truth through rigorous analysis and open dialogue.

Global Perspective

We prepare students to engage with diverse cultures and perspectives, equipping them to make meaningful contributions in an interconnected world.
